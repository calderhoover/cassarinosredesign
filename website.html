CASSARINO'S RISTORANTE REDESIGN

Adding a modern touch to Providence's historic Federal Hill

THE PROBLEM

Why redesign?

Cassarino's Ristorante is a renowned Italian restaurant in Federal Hill, the historic Italian district in Providence, Rhode Island. Though the restaurant remains busier than ever, it sports a website design that is as classic as the food it serves - and not in a good way. As a passionate regular at Cassarino's and a staunch supporter of the Providence food scene, I set out to bring a much needed burst of flavor to this tasteless interface.

CURRENT DESIGN

Where does Cassarino's currently stand?


Oversized text, empty space, and disorganized information make the site more difficult to navigate than it needs to be. Every page in the site requires scrolling, yet the site also requires clicking on tabs. This cycle of clicking, scrolling down a bit, then clicking again is tedious and frustrating for the user.

REDESIGN

What's a better way?

I reformed the site into a single scrollable interface, with tabs that remain at the top of the page after the user scrolls past the restaurant logo on the initial home screen. When a user clicks on a tab, the page scrolls down to that tab’s section. This way, it is clear that the user can access the entire site by both clicking on tabs and simply scrolling down, arriving at each section one by one. The sleeker design and intuitive navigation of this redesigned interface makes the site much more enjoyable to use. Overall, the site is less busy, with improved image and text organization, easy-to-identify content, and pleasurable spacing and delineation.

HOME PAGE

To save space, the address and phone number appear on the left and right sides respectively of the restaurant’s now-centered logo at the top of the homepage. The Home and About tabs are now combined into the Home tab, which reduces unnecessary whitespace and features dining hours and the “Make a reservation” button very clearly without having to scroll.

DIRECTIONS PAGE

The Directions page makes better use of space to feature both a Google Maps interface and a street view image of Cassarino's. Because the page is small, the start of the Daily Specials page (which the user can view fully by scrolling) can be seen.

DAILY SPECIALS PAGE

I added Daily Specials as a separate tab from the Home page to make sure that the user doesn’t miss that information. I also placed it in a more content appropriate spot than the Home page, after Directions and before Menu.

MENU PAGE

I made the Menu/Wine List tab into two separate tabs, and redesigned the Menu section. The Menu section now reads category by category from the top down, meaning that the user reads all appetizers, for example, before moving on to salads. Also, the categories are delineated with lines underlining them that go across the page. Overall, this makes categories and items within them very easy to identify and read. Items are also organized across 5 columns (5 items per “row”), making the text look much less messy.

CONTACT PAGE

The Contact page now features a street view image of Cassarino's, and organizes content into 3 neat columns.
